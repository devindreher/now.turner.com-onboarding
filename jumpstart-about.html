How we pay the bills.


Although each of our networks has unique content and programming, they all make money the same way — from a dual revenue stream powered by advertising sales and revenue generated by content license fees.

Content Sales

We sell our content to cable operators, satellite providers and telcos (e.g., AT&T). In turn, they package our product(s) and sell it as a subscription offering to their customers. Turner also licenses branded content from our networks and brands on emerging platforms such as smart phones and tablets, electronic storefronts (e.g., iTunes), and other video-on-demand outlets.

Advertising Sales

In addition to generating license fees, the distribution of content creates the audience foundation of Turner's ad sales business. Advertisers and ad agencies want their commercials seen by a particular viewing audience. The purchase price of this advertising is based on three factors:

Capacity:
The number of hours available to sell to advertisers.

Demographics:
Advertisers use this info to determine the type of audiences viewing a particular program.

Ratings
We use this info to sell advertising, set prices for commercials, attract media attention, and making programming decisions.
