Setting Up Your Password


Your hiring manager will provide you with a temporary password to get your started, but you'll need to create one of your own. Here's how:

  1. Visit change.timewarner.com.
  2. Enter your temporary password. Note that this password is case sensitive.
    Click "Log On" when finished.
  3. Create a new password of your choice using the following criteria:

    • Between 12 and 16 characters
    • Cannot contain your account name or parts of your full name that exceed 2 consecutive characters
    • Must contain characters from 3 of the following categories:
      English uppercase letters (A-Z)
      English lowercase letters (a-z)
      Base 10 digits (0–9)
      Non-alphanumeric characters (e.g., ! # $)
  4. Re-enter your new password in the "Confirm Password" field.
  5. Click "Submit" when finished.
 
 
 
 

Requesting an RSA Token


You have the option of having your RSA passcode (which gives you access to our network) on your mobile device (i.e., a "soft token") or as a key fob (i.e., a "hard token").

To request your token, contact the EIS/IT Service Desk. Once you receive your hard or soft token, follow these steps to access the network:

  1. Open the "Pulse Secure" application on your computer.
  2. Log in using your computer username and the passcode displayed on your token.
  3. You will be prompted to create a personalized pin. You pin must:

    • Be 6-8 characters in length — all numbers, all letters, or alphanumeric
    • Not use special characters, repeating numbers, or sequential numbers

Going forward, you will use your pin+token passcode to access the network.

Accessing Turner Wireless Networks


Turner employees worldwide needing wireless connectivity can connect their devices using the “tbsemployee” wireless network. For step-by-step instructions to connect your device to this network, visit mywirelessdevices.turner.com.

Turner guests and those traveling can may use the “WL-GUEST” and “twtraveler” wireless networks. Dial x9477 from an in-house phone to hear the GUEST wireless password for the day.

 
 
 
 

Email Retention & Document Management


Turner’s document management policy prescribes that employees should retain documents, including emails, only as long as they serve a business purpose. Therefore, all Turner employees should only retain email that is deemed business critical.

Emails in your inbox, sent items, and self-created email folders older than 60 days (from date sent/received) will be automatically deleted. To prevent business critical emails from automatic deletion and for more information, visit emailpolicy.turner.com.
